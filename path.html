How one rises

Ten ranks across four tiers, from Pledge to Chief Librarian.

Rank in the Assembly is earned, not granted. The Path of Ascension is written down so that no advancement is mysterious and no member is left guessing where they stand. Read it openly. Each rank below is described in its own words, and applies evenly to every keeper.

Low Ranks
I.
Pledge

Pledges are unofficial members of the division who have recently passed the Librarians' Assembly application. They have full access to the Librarian robes and team. Pledges have one week to achieve Student and be accepted into the Assembly as a bonafide member.

I do not know what they were waiting for me to write. I do not know what I was waiting for. The week passed. I had drafted three pieces, none I would have wanted read. None were read.· From a Pledge's correspondence, year unknown ·
II.
Student

Students are full-fledged members of the Librarians' Assembly. The first of the low ranks, they may begin working towards advancing in the Assembly.

III.
Librarian

Librarians are low-ranks of the Assembly who have begun to prove themselves as members. They may now gear up for the rank of Intellect.

Middle Ranks
IV.
Intellect

Intellects are the first official middle-rank of the Jedi Librarians' Assembly. They have gained enough knowledge and maturity to co-host training sessions.

V.
Tutor

Tutors are highly respected and have gained much knowledge within the Assembly. They are permitted to assist the High Ranks.

I had thought patience would be the difficulty. It was not. The difficulty was being needed unevenly. Two of my Pledges asked everything; one asked nothing; I gave the silent one too little and the others too much, and I knew I was doing it as I did it. I asked to be returned to study.· Reflection of a former Tutor ·
High Ranks
VI.
Lore Keeper

Lore Keepers are dedicated members devoted to the study and curation of the Jedi Archives. Strong scholars fulfilling the calling of wisdom and knowledge.

I made Lore Keeper in my fourth year and held the standing for eleven months. I taught no Pledges in that time, which is the licence the rank confers, and I read more than I had ever read. By the end of the eleventh month I had stopped wondering at any of it. The Curator did not refuse my request to return to Tutor. I was glad to have Pledges again.· Reflection of a former Lore Keeper ·
VII.
Archivist

Archivists are the Senior High Ranks of the Assembly. Held to the highest level of critical thinking and team management, they take charge in managing both the Assembly and the High Ranks, acting as mentors to all.

High Command
VIII.
Historian

The Historian is a highly respected rank within the Jedi Order, acting as a role model to those of lower rank. They assist the Curator in overseeing the High Ranks, and report directly to the Curator of the Archives and Chief Librarian.

Eight months. Long enough to know the work and to know I could not bear its weight without resentment. Better to step down well than to remain badly. The Assembly is not a stage; one need not exit on cue.· From a Historian's letter of resignation ·
IX.
Curator of the Archives

Curator of the Archives is the second-in-command of the Librarians' Assembly. This person directly oversees the High Ranks and reports to the Chief Librarian.

X.
Chief Librarian

Chief Librarian is the highest achievable rank, in first command of the Librarians' Assembly. This person oversees the Assembly, the Jedi Archives, and serves on the Council of First Knowledge.

I held the office for fourteen months. The Council asked me to remain. I declined. The Order is not preserved by those who cannot leave it. I trust my successor more than I trusted myself.· Vael Orin, 11th Chief Librarian, on stepping down ·
